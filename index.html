Capstone Project

Urban Parking Systems — Capstone

Systems Thinking · Primary Research · Behavioural Analysis
Redesigning Urban Parking — A Systems Approach
Mahindra University · Final Year Capstone · Team of 6
80+
Survey Responses
The Problem

What appeared to be a simple parking shortage problem revealed itself to be a deeply systemic issue — one driven by early occupancy, low turnover, concentrated demand, and complete absence of behavioral management. Urban parking fails not because of lack of space, but because of how existing space is structured and used.

Research Methodology

Conducted field visits across Hyderabad (Kukatpally, Ameerpet, Jubilee Hills, Banjara Hills, Secunderabad), 80+ survey responses, and 50 in-depth interviews. Combined primary behavioural data with secondary research on global parking management systems including dynamic pricing and demand-based platforms.

Key Insight

Cars are parked ~95% of the time — the real challenge is managing storage under scarcity. Supply-side solutions (adding more parking) induce more demand. Effective systems focus on demand management, better utilisation, and behavioural alignment through decision-support tools and nudges.

Arav's Contribution

Led the secondary research workstream — studied solutions from global cities including dynamic pricing models, shared parking platforms, and demand-based systems. Focused on breaking down the underlying logic of what makes these interventions work, and how they can be adapted to the Indian urban context. Helped synthesize team insights into a coherent strategic narrative.
